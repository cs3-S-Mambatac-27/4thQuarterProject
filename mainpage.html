Designing Robots

The process of designing robots has a lot of process, they are a lot of processes on designing robots so the robot can function well.
Here are the Steps of Designing a robot:
1. Define the plan
2. Research and brainstorm
3. Select and plan
4. Build and program
5. Test and evaluate
6. Refine and communicate
Designing a robot is a challenging and rewarding process that requires creativity, technical skills, and a systematic approach.

What are the Various applications of Designing and Programming Robots?

Robotics design and programming applications are found in a variety of industries, including space exploration, manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and agriculture. By automating procedures like welding, assembly, and material handling, industrial robots improve manufacturing processes' output and quality. Autonomous mobile robots in logistics and warehousing enhance order fulfillment and inventory management processes. Surgical robots are used in healthcare to perform precise operations, and assistive robots are used in rehabilitation and elder care. With its automated planting, harvesting, and weeding capabilities, agribots transform farming and increase food yields while promoting sustainability. While companion robots provide social and emotional support, service robots help humans in jobs like cleaning, security, and customer service. Space robots make it possible for missions to acquire information and carry out experiments while exploring far-off planets, moons, and asteroids. Students participate in practical STEM learning experiences through robotics education programs, which stimulate creativity.
